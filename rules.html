üéØ Game Overview

Imposter Game (also known as "Who is the Spy" or "Undercover") is a social deduction party game where players try to identify the impostor among them. Most players receive the same word (civilians), while one player receives a different but related word (the impostor).

Game Objective:
Civilians: Identify and vote out the impostor
Impostor: Blend in with civilians without being discovered

üë• Setup Requirements

  • Players: 3-12 people (optimal: 5-8)
  • Time: 5-15 minutes per round
  • Equipment: Just one device with our generator!
  • Age: 8+ (family-friendly)

üéÆ How to Play - Step by Step

Phase 1: Setup (2 minutes)

  1. Gather all players in a circle or around a table
  2. Choose a game category (Food, Animals, Places, Objects, Movies, or Jobs)
  3. Enter the number of players and their names in our generator
  4. Click "Start Game" - the generator will randomly assign:
    • One word to the civilians
    • A different (but related) word to the impostor

Phase 2: Word Reveal (1-2 minutes)

  1. Each player takes turns viewing their secret word privately
  2. Players must memorize their word (no screenshots!)
  3. Keep your word completely secret from others

üí° Pro Tip:

Pass the device around with the screen facing down. Each player taps "Reveal Word" when it's their turn, looks at it, then taps "Next Player" before passing.

Phase 3: Discussion Round (5-8 minutes)

  1. Round 1: Each player gives a brief description of their word WITHOUT saying the word directly
    • Example: If your word is "Pizza", you might say: "It's round, has cheese, often delivered"
  2. Round 2: Players can ask questions to each other
    • "Is yours commonly found in homes?"
    • "Would kids like yours?"
  3. Round 3: More detailed descriptions (optional)

‚ö†Ô∏è Important Rules:

  • Never say your exact word
  • Everyone must participate - no staying silent
  • No showing or writing your word
  • Listen carefully to others' descriptions

Phase 4: Voting (1 minute)

  1. After discussion, everyone votes for who they think is the impostor
  2. You can vote for anyone except yourself
  3. All players vote simultaneously (point fingers or use the app)
  4. The player with the most votes is eliminated

Phase 5: Results

  • If the impostor is voted out: Civilians win! üéâ
  • If a civilian is voted out: The impostor wins! üòà
  • Reveal both words to all players

üéØ Strategy Guide

For Civilians:

  • Be Specific Enough: Your description should prove you know the civilian word, but not give it away entirely
  • Watch for Vague Answers: The impostor will try to stay general - spot the pattern
  • Listen Carefully: Notice if someone's description doesn't quite fit
  • Build Trust: Use details that only someone with the same word would know
  • Coordinate: If you're confident someone else is a civilian, build on their descriptions

For the Impostor:

  • Listen First: Let others speak before you - this helps you understand the civilian word
  • Stay Generic: Use descriptions that could apply to many things in the category
  • Mirror Others: Copy the style and tone of civilian descriptions
  • Don't Panic: Stay calm when questioned - hesitation is suspicious
  • Deflect Suspicion: If questioned, turn it around: "Why are you asking that?"
  • Blend In: Don't be too quiet or too talkative - match the group energy

Limit discussion to 3 minutes total. Fast-paced and intense!

4. Multiple Impostors

For 8+ players, have 2 impostors with the same impostor word. They must identify each other subtly and work together.

‚ùì Common Questions

Can the impostor win without speaking?

No! Everyone must participate. Complete silence is against the rules and too suspicious.

What if two players get the same number of votes?

Have a second round of discussion (1 minute) focusing only on those two players, then vote again.

Can players team up and whisper?

No private conversations! All discussion must be public to the group.

What if someone accidentally says their word?

That player is immediately revealed and eliminated. If they're the impostor, civilians win. If civilian, continue playing.

Can we play online via video call?

Absolutely! Share your screen during word reveal, or each player can use the generator on their own device.

üåü Tips for Game Masters

  • Set a Timer: Keep discussions moving - don't let rounds drag on
  • Encourage Participation: Make sure quieter players get chances to speak
  • Start Easy: Use "Clue Word Mode" for first-time players
  • Mix Categories: Play multiple rounds with different categories
  • Keep Score: Track who has been impostor and won as civilian - it adds competitive fun!
